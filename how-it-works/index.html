The 14-day sprint, end to end.

Two weeks. Five phases. One number on paper.

The sprint is structured to compress the trial. Setup happens before day one. Launch is on day one. By day fifteen you have a clear hit or miss against the KPI you signed off on. Here's exactly what happens in each window.

The mechanic

From kickoff to decision in fifteen days.

Each phase has a defined job. No phase blends into the next. You always know what we're doing, what we're not doing yet, and why.

  1. Phase 01 of 05

    Days −3 to 0

    Setup. Before the spend starts.

    Kickoff call, ad-account access, brand guidelines, KPI agreement. We audit the existing funnel and the competitors who actually show up in your category. Everything gets built before a dollar of ad spend goes through the platform.

    • Kickoff call. We agree on the KPI and the win condition in writing.
    • Funnel audit. What you have today, what's converting, what's leaking.
    • Page rebuild draft. Hero, subhead, structure, copy.
    • VSL script draft (or rewrite of the existing one).
    • Ten-plus ad variations across cold and retargeting.
    • You approve everything before the first campaign goes live.

  2. Phase 02 of 05

    Days 1 to 4

    Launch and learning phase.

    Campaigns go live. Daily monitoring. No panic optimization while the algorithm is still gathering data. Touching the dials too early is how budgets get burned. You get a daily report so you see what we see.

    • Cold-traffic campaign goes live with five-plus creative variations.
    • Retargeting campaign goes live for warm visitors.
    • Daily report: spend, reach, click-through, cost-per-click, early lead signal.
    • No budget shifts during the learning phase. Discipline matters here.

  3. Phase 03 of 05

    Days 5 to 10

    Optimize. Kill, scale, iterate.

    Six days of active optimization. Kill the creative that isn't earning its impressions. Scale the variants that are pulling weight. Write and ship new creative against the early signal we now have. The campaign starts looking like itself.

    • Underperforming creative gets paused, not babied.
    • Winning variants get budget reallocation.
    • Iteration round: new hooks, new angles, fresh creative built against what's working.
    • Daily report continues. You stay in the loop without running the loop.

  4. Phase 04 of 05

    Days 11 to 14

    Final push and handoff prep.

    The last four days are about driving qualified volume into the KPI window. We're spending against what we've already proven works, not running new experiments. Post-sprint report goes together in parallel so the day-15 conversation is informed.

    • Spend concentrates on the proven winners.
    • Retargeting closes warm traffic the cold campaigns surfaced.
    • Post-sprint report drafted: what worked, what didn't, KPI status, recommendation.

  5. Phase 05 of 05

    Day 15

    Decision day. Hit or miss.

    We compare the result against the KPI you signed off on. There's no ambiguity here. The number is the number. If it hit, you pick Path A or Path B. If it missed, the engagement ends and you keep everything we built.

    • If hit: Path A (handoff, flat fee) or Path B (performance partnership). Your call.
    • If missed: Engagement ends. You keep the page, the ads, the VSL, the data. You owe nothing.
    • No automatic enrollment. No retainer trap. Two clean exits, by design.

Why fourteen days

Long enough to see signal. Short enough to keep skin in the game.

Fewer than ten days and the data is too noisy to read. More than three weeks and the engagement starts looking like a retainer with extra steps. Two weeks is the window where a real KPI becomes a real verdict, on both sides.
